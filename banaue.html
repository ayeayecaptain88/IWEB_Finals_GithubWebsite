The Eighth Wonder of the World

Carved by the Ifugao over 2,000 years ago, these magnificent terraces are a living heritage often called the "Eighth Wonder of the World." These ancient agricultural wonders represent one of humanity's greatest achievements in harmony with nature.

The Banaue Rice Terraces cover approximately 20,000 hectares of mountainous terrain in the Cordillera region. Built without modern machinery, these terraces demonstrate the ingenuity, skill, and deep environmental knowledge of the Ifugao people.

Sustainable Agriculture

The terraces practice crop rotation and natural pest control, supporting biodiversity for millennia.

Preservation Challenges

The terraces face modern challenges including climate change, outmigration of young people, and changing agricultural practices. Preserving this UNESCO World Heritage Site requires ongoing community support and sustainable tourism.
