Wonders of the Philippines

The Philippines is home to breathtaking landscapes and unique wonders worth discovering. From its rich cultural heritage to natural marvels that define its islands.

Cultural Heritage

Banaue Rice Terraces

Living heritage that dates back 2,000 years, showcasing the skill and tradition of the Ifugao people.

Cultural Heritage

Chocolate Hills

These over a thousand cone-shaped hills turn brown in summer, resembling mounds of chocolate.

Cultural Heritage

Batad Rice Terraces

Carved into the mountains over 2,000 years ago, Batad is a UNESCO-recognized heritage of Ifugao engineering.

Cultural Heritage

Tubbataha Reefs

A UNESCO World Heritage Site teeming with diverse marine life and pristine coral reefs.

Cultural Heritage

Puerto Princesa Underground River

Known for its limestone karst landscapes and the world’s second-longest underground river.

Cultural Heritage

Hundred Islands

A natural playground of 124 islands scattered across Lingayen Gulf.

Cultural Heritage

Taal Volcano & Lake

A volcano within a lake, within a larger volcano—an iconic natural wonder unique to the Philippines.

Cultural Heritage

Mt. Apo

The tallest peak in the Philippines and a center of rich biodiversity and culture.

Cultural Heritage

Taal Volcano & Lake

A volcano within a lake, within a larger volcano—an iconic natural wonder unique to the Philippines.

Experience Philippine’s Natural Heritage

Each wonder reveals a story of nature’s incredible biodiversity, cultural heritage, and geological marvels. From UNESCO World Heritage Sites to hidden gems, these destinations showcase the timeless magic of the islands.
