Nature's Perfect Architecture

Famous for its near-perfect cone shape, Mayon Volcano stands as one of the Philippines' most iconic landmarks. This active stratovolcano in Albay Province is renowned worldwide for its remarkable symmetry and breathtaking beauty.

Rising to 2,463 meters above sea level, Mayon is both majestic and unpredictable. Its perfect conical form has been shaped by centuries of volcanic activity, making it a geological wonder and a symbol of the Philippines' natural power.

Geological Marvel

Mayon's perfect cone shape is the result of consistent basaltic-andesitic eruptions that have built up its symmetrical profile over thousands of years. This geological consistency is rare among the world's volcanoes.

The volcano has erupted more than 50 times in recorded history, with its most destructive eruption occurring in 1814. Despite its beauty, Mayon remains an active and closely monitored volcano.
